Strong’s Concordance assigns a unique number to many Hebrew and Greek words in the Bible, making it easier to study the original terms, their meanings, and where they appear in Scripture. -AI

About Strong’s Concordance

In this site, Strong’s numbers act as a bridge between English translations and the underlying Hebrew and Greek words, so you can move from a verse to the original term, its basic gloss, and related occurrences. -AI

Each Strong’s entry page provides the Strong’s number, the lemma (original-language form), a brief gloss, and links or references that can be expanded in future versions of the project. -AI

Strong’s Hebrew Lexicon (H-numbers)

The Hebrew Strong’s lexicon covers words from the Old Testament, which was primarily written in Biblical Hebrew (with some Aramaic), a consonant-rich Semitic language used by ancient Israel. -AI

Each Hebrew entry is labeled with an H-number (e.g., H0001) and links the English translation back to a specific Hebrew lemma, providing a starting point for word studies and cross-references. -AI

Strong’s Greek Lexicon (G-numbers)

The Greek Strong’s lexicon covers words from the New Testament, written in Koine Greek, the common dialect of the eastern Mediterranean world during the first century. -AI

Each Greek entry is labeled with a G-number (e.g., G0001) and connects English translations to their underlying Greek lemmas, helping you trace how a term is used across different passages. -AI

Using Strong’s on this site

  • Follow Strong’s numbers from Bible tools or verse views to open the corresponding word entry and see its lemma and basic gloss. -AI
  • Use the Hebrew and Greek index pages to browse or search for specific H- or G-numbers when doing more focused word studies. -AI
  • As the project grows, additional links, references, and cross-tools will be added to make Strong’s-based study faster and more connected. -AI
