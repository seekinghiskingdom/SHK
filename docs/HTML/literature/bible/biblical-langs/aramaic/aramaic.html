Biblical Aramaic appears in select Old Testament passages and in a few preserved sayings in the New Testament, reflecting the everyday language of many Jews in the late Old Testament and Second Temple periods. -AI

Overview

Closely related to Hebrew, Aramaic shares many structural features but has its own vocabulary and expressions, showing up in key narrative and prophetic sections as well as in certain quoted phrases in the Gospels. -AI

On this site, Aramaic resources will focus on highlighting the passages where Aramaic occurs, offering basic lexical information, and connecting those words to the surrounding Hebrew and Greek context. -AI
